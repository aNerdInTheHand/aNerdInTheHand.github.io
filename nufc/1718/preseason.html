Preseason... a tantalising taste of what's to come, but never quite the real deal.

Supposedly an opportunity for all the new signings to gel into the team, but in these days of JIM WHITE SHOUTING DAY, aka TRANSFER DEADLINE DAY, it seems unfashionable to sign anybody before you're three games into the season. That's maybe a little bit unfair, as we got 5 in - plus a few kids - before the first league game, and as I write have subsequently added Joselu. By and large I don't think any of them are bad signings, but by Rafa's own admission there aren't many of his first choices amongst them.

Christian Atsu ended last season well and general consensus amongst my mates is that it was a no brainer to sign him for the price (a rumoured £6m or thereabouts, or 1/33 Neymars in modern currency). His early season form (I'm writing this after the West Ham game having decided on a whim to start this blog) suggests he could well be a bargain.

We then completed the signing of Florian Lejeune after about 10 years of negotiations, presumably along the lines of:
"We'll give you €5m."
"The price is €8m."
"We'll give you €5,000,001."
"The price is €8m."
"We'll give you €5,000,002."
You get the picture... However, we did manage to get this former Man City world domination pawn in in time to look excellent in pre-season and the first 30 minutes of the Premier League season before getting nobbled by Kane.

Next in was Jacob Murphy in a deal that seemed to follow the Lejeune negotiating tactics. It was around this time it became apparent that we somehow had no money - missing the first year of squillion pound Premier League TV money seems to have hurt us more than we realised, which will no doubt perversely delight Mike Ashley as he takes to television to tell us deluded Geordies that he sadly can't compete with Bruce Wayne, Montgomery Burns, Scrooge McDuck and Smaug like we keep asking him to. Oh, you said Bournemouth? Well, we can't compete with them either.

To confirm this point, we then signed Javier Manquillo in a move that delighted our friends from wearside following his disappointing loan spell with them last year. However, a bit of digging seems to reveal that David Moyes played his part in Javier's form last year, hooking him after his best performance of the season and rarely playing him after that. Rafa cited his potential, his flexibility (he can play across the back four) and his penchant for chorizo and Sangria as reasons for his signing, and he's looked much as we might have expected in the early part of his Toon career.

An interesting one next in the form of Mikel Merino from Dortmund, on a promise (a loan with an obligation to buy him when our dole comes in). I freely admit I didn't know much about him, and he didn't play much at Dortmund last year, but he featured for Spain in the U21 Euros, picking up a runners up medal, and that's pedigree enough for me. He looked decent in pre-season and has bags of talent, although the first couple of defeats suggest he might need a little while to toughen up and adjust to the Premier League. Definitely an exciting one to watch though.

Hands up who had Joselu Mato top of their striker wishlist? Hands down in the back, you lying bugger... Signed out of pre-season for £5m from Stoke reserves, I've got to admin he looks capable of being the player he was for me on Footy Manager 14! I'm writing this the day after the West Ham game where he got his first goal but also breathed life into the team by knowing an archaic rule, long forgotten in these parts, of being able to HOLD THE BALL UP, thus bringing his team mates into play.
